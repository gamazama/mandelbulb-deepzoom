Mandelbulb Perturbation Deep-Zoom

Real-time fly-navigation of the 3D Mandelbulb to ~1e-50 surface distance on a consumer GPU (WebGL2), via 3D perturbation theory. A literature sweep (June 2026) found no published derivation, code, or demonstration of Mandelbulb perturbation deep-zoom; prior renderers wall at ~1e-6 (f32) / ~1e-13 (f64). Every depth milestone is certified against an independent arbitrary-precision referee that shares no code or math with the renderer.

Standing on the shoulders of

  • The Mandelbulb (triplex power-8 map) — Daniel White & Paul Nylander, with the fractalforums community (2009).
  • Perturbation theory for deep zoom — K. I. Martin, SuperFractalThing (2013).
  • Distance-estimated ray marching — John C. Hart et al. (sphere tracing, 1996); Mandelbulb DE practice popularized by Inigo Quilez and Mikael Hvidtfeldt Christensen (Syntopia). The cosine colour palette is Quilez's.
  • Rebasing & bilinear approximation (BLA) for perturbation — Zhuoran (fractalforums, 2021–22); developed further in Claude Heiland-Allen's Fraktaler-3 and the wider deep-zoom community (FractalShark, Imagina, FractalShades; mathr.co.uk).
  • Glitch-detection lineage — Pauldelbrot (fractalforums).
  • 2D deep-zoom architecture, HDR float helpers, fly-slowdown — ported from GMT (gmt-fractals.com), the GPL-3.0 explorer this extends.

Project

Gigh Zack — project lead, GMT. Posed the problem, set the 1e-50 target, and flew every acceptance flight (first human to navigate the Mandelbulb at 1e-20). Diagnosed by eye three renderer defects that all automated suites had missed — the first-order-Δθ formulation bug, the march overshoot, and the render-resolution hit epsilon — the corrections that made the geometry true. Feature direction from Lycium (Maxon): local camera lighting, camera-path export.

Built with Claude Code (Anthropic, June 2026)

Claude Opus 4.x — prep & consolidation: the exact triplex Jacobian and the cancellation-free delta recurrence; the transcendental-free (Chebyshev) high-precision reference orbit; the CPU validation suites, the BigInt direct referee, the GPU BLA port, the march-population study. Claude Fable 5 — research: that double-single arithmetic is dead on NVIDIA (EFT collapse) → HDR mantissa+exponent δ; found and fixed the two O(1) cancellations and the first-order Δθ (root cause of the depth wall); built the renderer; designed the certification methodology and the triplex BLA theory.

Methodology rule, twice earned here: implementation-agreement tests are circular for formulation bugs — every certification keeps one referee that shares zero math with the kernel.

What is new here, to our knowledge

An exact, cancellation-free perturbation recurrence for the non-analytic triplex power (including the exact Δθ/Δφ residual forms); a perturbed distance-estimator delta (Δr/Δdr) valid to 1e-50; BLA skip tables generalized to a 3D non-analytic map; and the certified-depth methodology.

Substrate

WebGL2 / GLSL ES 3.00 via ANGLE (d3d11, gl, vulkan — all verified) · HDR (mantissa f32 + int exponent) δ, no error-free transforms · BigInt fixed-point (P=256, ~77 digits) reference orbits and camera, transcendental-free via Chebyshev · per-frame reference rebuild at the camera · Zhuoran-style conditional rebasing with Δdr re-anchor · real-GPU test harness with f64 and BigInt direct referees, 11/11 certified 1e-5 → 1e-50.
